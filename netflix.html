Netflix Nights

It starts with something soft.
Something comforting.

Like The Life List or The Oxford Year—
stories where people somehow figure things out
in the most aesthetic way possible.

I watch her move to a new country,
fall in love,
heal parts of herself
she didn’t even know were broken.

And I sit there thinking—
maybe I need a new city too.
maybe I need a new life.

But instead of packing my bags,
I just click “Next Episode.”

Then it shifts.

Something heavier.
Something real.

Like Normal People
or 13 Reasons Why.

Stories where people don’t have it together.
Where love is messy.
Where silence says more than words ever could.

And suddenly,
it’s not just a show anymore.

It’s the texts I didn’t send.
The conversations I avoided.
The feelings I keep pretending I don’t have.

I pause for a second.

Not because I’m bored—
but because it feels a little too familiar.

Then I switch again.

Because staying there too long
is uncomfortable.

Now it’s something distracting.
Fast-paced. Addictive.

Like Money Heist
or Stranger Things.

Where everything is intense,
everything matters,
and every second feels important.

Unlike my own life,
which feels like it’s stuck buffering.

In their world,
they’re risking everything.

In mine,
I’m avoiding everything.

And somehow,
watching them makes me feel like
I’m doing something.

Even if I’m just lying in bed,
in the same position for hours.

“Are you still watching?”

Yes.

But not just the show.

I’m watching other people
live the kind of lives
I keep postponing.

People fall in love.
They take risks.
They leave.
They begin again.

All in one night.

And I?

I stay.

In the same room.
With the same thoughts.

Telling myself—
tomorrow I’ll start.
tomorrow I’ll fix things.
tomorrow I’ll be different.

But tomorrow
always begins
after one more episode.

Because Netflix Nights
were never just about shows.

They’re about escape.
About feeling something
without having to do anything about it.

It’s easier to watch someone
choose a new life…

than to choose your own.

And maybe one day,
I’ll stop clicking “Next Episode.”

Maybe one day,
I’ll become the kind of story
I keep watching.

But not tonight.

Tonight,
I’m still here.

Watching.
Waiting.
Ignoring.

“Next Episode starts in 5.. 4..3..2..1..0 seconds…”
