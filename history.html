If My Thoughts Had a Search History

A quiet timeline of a restless night • Pranjal Vats

It would probably start small.
Harmless. Forgettable.

Like nothing was ever wrong.

12:07 AM
why can’t I sleep even when I’m tired

The room is quiet.
But my head isn’t.

The fan hums like it knows something I don’t.
And suddenly, silence feels louder than noise.

12:43 AM
how to stop overthinking everything

I turn to my side.
Then the other.
Then back again.

It’s funny—
my body wants rest,
but my mind wants answers.

1:26 AM
do people notice when you slowly stop talking

There was a time I filled every silence.
Now I sit inside it.

No one asks.
Or maybe they do—
just not enough to wait for a real answer.

2:11 AM
is it normal to feel left behind in life

I scroll.
Everyone seems to be moving somewhere.

Forward.
Upward.
Anywhere but here.

And I’m stuck measuring my worth
in comparison to people
who don’t even know I exist like this.

2:58 AM
how to disappear for a while without hurting anyone

I don’t mean vanish.

Just… pause.

Step out of the version of me
everyone expects me to be.

But even that feels selfish.

3:37 AM
when did I stop feeling like myself

This one stays open the longest.

No quick answers.
No neat articles.

Just a quiet realization—
it didn’t happen all at once.

It was gradual.

A little silence here.
A little pretending there.
A few swallowed words.
A few ignored feelings.

Until one day,
I couldn’t recognize the person
who knew how to feel everything so deeply.

4:12 AM
why do I ruin things that actually matter

Because staying feels risky.

Because losing something slowly
hurts more than ending it early.

Because sometimes,
I’d rather be the reason it breaks
than the one left wondering why it did.

4:55 AM
how to become someone I’d be proud of

This one feels different.

Quieter.
Less desperate.
More… honest.

Like I’m not trying to escape anymore—
just trying to understand.

Morning comes anyway.
It always does.

The world wakes up
like nothing inside me stayed awake all night.

And if my thoughts really had a search history,
I’d probably clear it.

Delete everything. Start fresh.
Pretend none of it happened.

But maybe that’s the problem.

Maybe it’s not meant to be erased.

Maybe it’s meant to be read—
slowly, carefully,
without skipping the uncomfortable parts.

Because somewhere between
“why am I like this”
and
“how do I change”

there’s a version of me
still waiting to be understood.

how to be okay… not all at once, just a little at a time
