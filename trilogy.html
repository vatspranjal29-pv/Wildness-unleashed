Time walks with silent, endless feet, A conqueror no soul can meet. It crowns the child, it bends the old, It buys with silver, it sells with gold. It hides in laughter, it sleeps in tears, It shapes the ages, it buries years. The strongest fall, the proud decay, Time carves its mark and walks away. We squander hours as though they stay, But moments vanish, lost in clay. The careless drift, the dreamers sigh, While Time records and passes by. Yet those who wake, who pierce its veil, Know every second tips the scale. They guard their days, they spend, not lose, They shape the path their hands must choose.
