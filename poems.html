बस इंसान होना

हम बहुत मज़बूत नहीं हैं,
बस हालातों से हार नहीं मानते।
थक जाते हैं, टूटते भी हैं,
पर रुक जाना नहीं जानते।
हम बहुत मज़बूत नहीं हैं,
बस हालातों से हार नहीं मानते।
थक जाते हैं, टूटते भी हैं,
पर रुक जाना नहीं जानते।

हर दिन ख़ुद को समझाते हैं,
कि सब ठीक हो जाएगा कभी।
यक़ीन कम होता है आजकल,
पर उम्मीद छोड़ते नहीं अभी।

हम हँसते हैं क्योंकि ज़रूरी है,
ना कि हर बात में ख़ुशी होती है।
कुछ मुस्कानें ज़िम्मेदारी होती हैं,
हर हँसी में कहानी होती है।

कभी–कभी चुप रह जाना ही बेहतर है,
हर सच को कहना भी बोझ बन जाता है।
इंसान होने की यही क़ीमत है शायद,
कि जीना भी एक काम बन जाता है।
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

🌬️ हवा का पीछा

हवा का पीछा कौन करे,
वो तो बस महसूस होती है।
कभी गालों को छू जाती है,
कभी आँखों में धूल बोती है।
हवा का पीछा कौन करे,
वो तो बस महसूस होती है।
कभी गालों को छू जाती है,
कभी आँखों में धूल बोती है।

मैं भी निकली थी एक दिन,
हवा के संग उड़ जाने को,
पर हर मोड़ पर वो मुड़ जाती,
मुझे राहें समझाने को।

कभी वो पत्तों में हँसती थी,
कभी समंदर में गाती थी,
मैं सोचती रही पकड़ लूँ उसको,
पर वो हर बार मुस्कुराती थी।

अब समझ आया —
हवा को पकड़ा नहीं जाता,
बस जीया जाता है,
जैसे ख्वाबों का एक टुकड़ा,
जो पलभर में बीत जाता है।

हवा का पीछा मत कर,
उसमें बहना सीख ले,
कभी तूफ़ान बन,
कभी शांति बन —
पर जीना हवा की तरह सीख ले।
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

Somewhere between Dusk and Dawn: Dawn arrives on golden wings, A whispered hope the sunrise brings. Yet in its glow, the battle starts,
Somewhere between Dusk and Dawn: Dawn arrives on golden wings, A whispered hope the sunrise brings. Yet in its glow, the battle starts, A restless fire within my heart. The hours stretch, the weight is vast Dreams pull forward, fears hold fast With every step, I rise and break A war within, for fate’s own sake The sun hangs high, the toil grows deep, I chase my dreams, I lose my sleep The road is long, the path unclear Yet still, I march through doubt and fear Then dusk descends, in hues of red A canvas, where my scars are spread The night arrives to heal, to mend, Yet, dawn awaits to start again…..

Will I Regret...

Will I regret the times I stalled, The chances missed, the dreams I called— Then left unheard, beneath my doubt, Too scared to scream, too worn to shout?
Will I regret the work half-done, The races lost I could have won? The lazy days, the fleeting fire, The faded edge of once desire? I tell myself, "You'll make it still," But time won't pause for fading will. Each hour that slips through idle hands, Could build the life my soul demands. Will I regret not giving all, When I still had the strength to fall— And rise again, with dirt and grace, Instead of quitting at half pace? But maybe… maybe I will learn, That purpose isn't always stern. That stumbles count, that trying slow, Still leads somewhere—still helps me grow. So will I regret? The fear runs deep, But I’ll build something I can keep. Not perfect paths or flawless days— But truth I lived in flawed, real ways.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

🚇 मेट्रो का सफ़र

हर रोज़ मेट्रो में सफ़र करती हूँ,
पर लगता है जैसे कोई कहीं पहुँचता ही नहीं।
भीड़ है, शोर है, दौड़ है—
पर हर चेहरा अपनी खामोशी में क़ैद।
हर रोज़ मेट्रो में सफ़र करती हूँ,
पर लगता है जैसे कोई कहीं पहुँचता ही नहीं।
भीड़ है, शोर है, दौड़ है—
पर हर चेहरा अपनी खामोशी में क़ैद।

किसी की आँखों में नींद की राख,
किसी की मुस्कान में आधा सच,
किसी की उँगलियों में फोन की पकड़—
जैसे वही एक नाता बचा हो दुनिया से।

लोग ज़िंदगी नहीं जी रहे,
बस कंधों पर ढो रहे हैं—
फ़िक्रें, फ़र्ज़ और फ़ोन की रोशनी।

और मैं सोचती हूँ…
कभी हम फिर दिल से जियेंगे भी,
या सिर्फ़ चलती मेट्रो की तरह चलते ही रहेंगे?
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

The Ravages of Time ..Part I. Whispers of the Hourglass 🌌

Time walks with silent, endless feet, A conqueror no soul can meet. It crowns the child, it bends the old, It buys with silver, it sells with gold.
Time walks with silent, endless feet, A conqueror no soul can meet. It crowns the child, it bends the old, It buys with silver, it sells with gold. It hides in laughter, it sleeps in tears, It shapes the ages, it buries years. The strongest fall, the proud decay, Time carves its mark and walks away. We squander hours as though they stay, But moments vanish, lost in clay. The careless drift, the dreamers sigh, While Time records and passes by. Yet those who wake, who pierce its veil, Know every second tips the scale. They guard their days, they spend, not lose, They shape the path their hands must choose. For though Time strips both crown and throne, It bows to those who make it known: That life is brief, yet vast, divine— And they are masters of their time.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

⏳ Next Up

You’ve been living life like a timetable.

Always the next box to tick.
Next exam.
Next class.
Next year.
You’ve been living life like a timetable.

Always the next box to tick.
Next exam.
Next class.
Next year.
Next entrance.
Next college.
Next semester.
Next placement.
Next job.

A future that keeps arriving
only to be replaced
by another future.

Your days are neatly folded
into planners and deadlines,
dreams converted
into five-year strategies,
breaths taken
between “what next?”
and “what after that?”

And somewhere—
between the mock tests
and the counselling rounds,
between the cut-offs
and career graphs,
between becoming
and becoming—

you forgot
how it feels
to just be.

To run
without measuring the distance.
To choose a road
that doesn’t end
in a result.

Tell me—

when do we explore?
When do we get lost on purpose?
When do we chase sunsets
instead of ranks?

Or are we meant
to spend our whole lives
reaching destinations
we never paused
to want?

Maybe
not every step
needs a reason.

Maybe
some days
are meant
to be wild—
aimless,
unmapped,
unplanned—

like wind
that never asked
where it was going
before it learned
how to fly.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

The Ravages of Time ..Part II. The Sculptor’s Hands 🪶

Time is a sculptor, patient, cold, It shapes the young, it breaks the old. It writes in wrinkles, it inks in bone, A story etched, yet never shown.
Time is a sculptor, patient, cold, It shapes the young, it breaks the old. It writes in wrinkles, it inks in bone, A story etched, yet never shown. It waits in silence, sharp, unseen, It turns today to what has been. The fleeting hour, the wasted breath, All march in step toward certain death. Kings have begged, and poets prayed, But Time unmoved, has never swayed. It eats the empires, grinds the stone, And leaves the dreamer all alone. Yet wisdom blooms for those aware, Who treat each moment as treasure rare. They rise above, they seize, they climb, And live unbroken by Time’s crime. For Time may rule, but still it yields To hearts that harvest their own fields. Though fleeting, fragile, brief, sublime— The undefeated conquer Time.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

The Ravages of Time ..Part III. The Predator’s Chime 🕯️

Time is a predator, sharp and slow, It hunts us all, above, below. It gnaws at bone, it drinks our youth, It strips away all but the truth.
Time is a predator, sharp and slow, It hunts us all, above, below. It gnaws at bone, it drinks our youth, It strips away all but the truth. Castles crumble, empires fade, Dreams turn to dust, foundations laid. Time takes the victor, thief, and sage, It locks them all within its cage. It does not bargain, does not plead, It answers none, it knows no need. The mighty beg, the fragile cry, Yet all must kneel as hours pass by. It haunts the mirror, cold and near, It whispers loss into the ear. A steady hand, unseen, supreme, Unraveling life, unweaving dream. And yet the few who face its gaze, Who live their hours, who burn their days, They do not fear the clock’s grim chime— For they are victors over Time.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

Like Salt 🧂

I am like salt, simple and small, Yet I blend in quietly, touching all. In every dish, in every soul, I never seek to take control.
Too little of me—you’ll feel the lack, A silent space, a subtle crack. Too much—and I’ll sting the tongue, A harmony lost, a song unsung. I walk with fire, laugh with rain, Ease your joy, and soothe your pain. I don’t need to shine or lead, Just be the part that others need. From the richest plate to the poorest fare, You’ll find my spirit lingering there. Not loud or proud, but always true— A silent strength that carries through. I shift, I mold, I find my place, In every heart, in every space. Call me humble, or call me wise— I am salt—small, but I rise.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

Clockwork Days ⏰

Tick-tock, tick-tock — the rhythm never sways, I rise and fall in endless, numbered days. A cog inside a giant, silent wheel, No room to laugh, no time to feel.
Same street, same step, same weary breath, Each hour chimes a little death. Eyes on the screen, hands to the task, Beneath this skin, I wear a mask. No curveballs thrown, no sudden skies, Just blinking lights and tired eyes. Coffee, calls, and empty talk, Life reduced to a daily walk. No dance, no detour, no surprise, Just routine dressed in a thin disguise. A robot’s grace, a rusted soul, Marching on to someone else’s goal. I dream of mess, of wild delight, Of losing track of day and night. Of roads unplanned, of skies unknown, Of moments I can call my own. But still I tick, I tock, I turn, A silent ache, a buried yearn. For now, I move where I'm supposed to be, But deep inside — I long to break free.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

What Will Happen? 🤔

I'm trying, yes, but not my best, A silent war inside my chest. I know the cost, I know the stakes, Still find myself making mistakes.
I want to rise, I want to prove, But some days I just cannot move. When fire burns, I give my all, When it goes dim, I slip, I fall. Motivation—fickle friend, Here one day, then meets its end. When it’s strong, I scale the sky, When it fades, I barely try. I ask myself, “What will this mean?” Will I be lost in might-have-beens? Will dreams decay in silent rust, All for a lack of constant trust? But deep inside, beneath the doubt, A voice still whispers, strong and loud: “You are not made of perfect days, But strength is shown in flawed displays.” So what will happen? Time will say, But I will rise in my own way.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

I Am the Storm 🌪️

I was the quiet one, eyes full of flame, Now they all shiver when they hear my name. Mocked my dreams? Well, joke’s on you— I built a throne from the shade you threw.
Didn’t fit in? I never tried. Kings don’t blend, they multiply pride. Now I walk in with that “don’t-care” grin, And a fire so fierce, I burn to win. Friends turned rivals, haters in disguise, Watch me rise while they rehearse lies. I don’t flex cheap—I let my life scream, Luxury’s my silence, success is my scream. Call it revenge? I call it fate. You doubted gold ‘cause you saw it in slate. Now I shine like a war god’s crown, Watch me buy what once brought me down. No more limits, no second guess, I’m the calm chaos in tailored finesse. Spent too long being their maybe, their "not quite”— Now I’m first-class flying into every fight. I’m the answer to every eye that rolled, The living proof that stories unfold Not in their hands, not in their say— But in the silence of those who slay. You want humble? That phase is dead. Now I live bold, paint the skyline red. I'm not just rich—I'm out of your league, A walking revenge in designer intrigue. So when they ask, “How did you win?” I’ll smirk and say, “By never giving in.” Let my glow blind every fake smile swarm— I didn’t chase the light. I became the storm.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

Salt in My Soul 🧂

I’m not the flame that steals the scene, Nor the velvet in a royal sheen. I’m quiet strength, not loud or bold, But without me, the story feels cold.
In laughter's feast or silence deep, In moments loud or ones you keep, I’m there—unseen but understood, The touch that turns bland into good. I sit with joy, I walk with grief, I bring a pause, I grant relief. I never beg, I never boast, But I become what you need most. With strangers, I am light and kind, With friends, a peace they’re glad to find. In chaos, I can hold the ground, In stillness, I can wear the crown. Adaptive, yes—yet still my core, Unchanged though I’m asked for more. I bend, but never disappear, I'm felt the most when I'm sincere. So taste and see—don’t rush or shove, I’m best when measured, shared with love. Not too much, nor barely there, Just enough—and I’m everywhere.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

Running on Empty 🕯️

I used to wake with fire in my chest, Now it’s just weight that won’t let me rest. My calendar's full, but my soul feels thin, Like I’m chasing a race I forgot to begin.
Smiles worn like practiced art, But the cracks run deep beneath my heart. “Keep going,” they cheer — I nod, comply, Yet every breath feels like a lie. The to-dos scream louder than my needs, I water others, forget my own seeds. Eyes wide open, but the light is dim, Even silence feels like it’s wearing thin. It’s not that I’m weak, or don’t want to try, I’m just tired in ways no sleep can defy. A soul stretched thin, a mind worn out, An inner scream behind a whisper shout. But maybe rest is not defeat, Maybe pause is what makes strength complete. I’ll learn to breathe, not just survive— To stop, to heal, to feel alive.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻

The Pause ⏸️

I gave and gave, then gave some more, Till I forgot what I was doing it for. Each win felt empty, joy ran dry, Even the sunrise passed me by.
They called me strong, they called me brave, But didn’t see the pieces I gave. Behind the work, beneath the grin, I barely knew the skin I’m in. Then one day—I didn’t rise. Just laid there, staring at the skies. And in that stillness, something stirred: A softer voice I hadn’t heard. “You don’t have to climb today. It’s okay to just float halfway.” And in the quiet, I found my start— Not in the doing, but in the heart.
— By Pranjal Vats 🌻
